Course information

COMP 2680 Introduction to Programming

This course provides a foundation in programming concepts using a high-level language (e.g., Python or Java).
Students learn about variables, control structures,
functions, and basic algorithms. Designed for beginners, it emphasizes problem-solving and logical thinking.

COMP 2210 Data Structures and Algorithms

An intermediate course focused on essential data structures (like arrays, linked lists, stacks, queues, trees, and graphs) and algorithms.
Topics include sorting, searching, and algorithm complexity.
Emphasis is placed on efficiency, performance, and code organization.

MATH 101 Calculus I

This introductory calculus course covers limits, derivatives, and integrals of single-variable functions.
Applications include optimization, motion, and area under curves.
It's foundational for science, engineering, and computer science majors.

FILM 1180 Introduction to Film Studies

A survey course exploring the history, aesthetics, and cultural impact of film.
Students learn how to analyze cinematic techniques, genres, and storytelling methods.
Films from various eras and regions are studied in a critical context.
